Izuku Midoriya's Japanese Katsu Curry

Description

This is an anime-inspired recipe from our local hero-in-training Izuku Midoriya (My Hero Academia), who's favorite food is a pork cutlet bowl. However, instead of pork, it will be with chicken.

Ingredients

Servings: 2-4

Chicken Katsu

  • 1 lb chicken breast/thighs
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 2 cups Panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil or vegetable oil

Curry

  • 3 cups of water
  • 1 cup of chicken stock
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2-3 small potatoes, cubed
  • 3-4 carrots, chopped
  • 1 package of curry box
  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil or vegetable oil

Optional

  • 1 cup of cooked white rice

Instructions

Chicken Katsu

  1. Heat up oil in pan in medium-high heat.
  2. While oil is heating up, thin chicken breasts or thighs with a mallet.
  3. In a medium bowl, mix flour, Panko breadcrumbs, salt, and pepper.
  4. In a small, separate bowl, lightly whisk the eggs.
  5. Take chicken breasts or thighs and coat it in the egg mixture.
  6. Next, coat the chicken in the flour and Panko mixture. Repeat until all chicken has been coated with the mixture.
  7. Fry the chicken in pan. Adjust to a lower temperature if the pan starts smoking.
  8. Flip chicken when one side has turned golden brown.
  9. Chicken is done when the other side has turned golden brown and the internal temperature has reached 160 degrees Fahrenheit.

Curry

  1. Heat up oil in pan in medium heat.
  2. Toss in onion onto pan and saute until transculent.
  3. Toss in potatoes, carrots, chicken stock, and water. Let it simmer until vegetables are fully cooked.
  4. Toss in the curry cubes and stir until dissolved.
  5. Optional: In a bowl of white rice, add curry and then chicken katsu once done.
